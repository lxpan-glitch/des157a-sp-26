Overlays Design Pattern Research

April 23, 2026

Reading about modals and overlays made me rethink how often I have seen them used in everyday apps without really questioning their purpose. Before this, I mostly thought of modals as just a design pattern to display extra information, but the article emphasized that they actually interrupt the user’s flow and demand attention. That made me realize that using a modal is not just a visual decision, but a behavioral one.

What stood out to me most was the idea that modals should only be used for critical actions, such as confirmations, important inputs, or situations where the user really needs to focus on one task. I have seen many websites use overlays for promotions, newsletter popups, or unnecessary messages, and now those interactions feel more intrusive than helpful. A modal should support the user’s goal, not become another obstacle between the user and the content.

Another important part was accessibility. Details like a clear close button, keyboard interaction, and not trapping users inside the modal seem small, but they can completely change the experience. This made me reflect on my own JavaScript overlay work. I realized that I was mostly focused on making the overlay function visually, but I also need to think about whether the interaction feels clear, respectful, and easy to exit.

Research From Design

April 14, 2026

After reading this article, I started thinking about forms a little differently. Before, I usually saw them as a basic part of a website that you just fill out and move past. I did not really pay attention to how much the design of a form can shape the whole experience.

What stood out to me most was the idea that a form should make things easier for the user, not make the task feel heavier. When a page is too crowded or the steps are not clear, even a simple action starts to feel annoying. That made me realize that good form design is not only about collecting information. It is also about helping people stay focused and not feel lost while they are trying to finish something.

One site I use often is MyUCDavis. Looking at it again after reading the article, I noticed that some parts feel busy and harder to read than they need to be. There is a lot happening on the screen at once, so it can take extra time to figure out where to look first. Compared to the article, I think the experience would feel better if the layout were calmer and the information were guided in a clearer way.
